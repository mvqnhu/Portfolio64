    Key insights:

    1. First purchase behavior:

  • Customer A's first item was sushi
  • Customer B's was curry
  • customer C's was ramen

    • 2. Overall best-selling item:

  • Ramen is the most purchased item (8 times total).

    • 3. Customer-specific favorite items:

  • Customer A and B both ordered sushi the most.
  • Customer C favored ramen.

    • 4. Behavior related to membership:

  • After becoming a member: Customer A first purchased curry; Customer B first purchased sushi.
  • Just before becoming a member: Customer A bought sushi and curry; Customer B bought sushi.

    • Overall insights:

  • Sushi and ramen are key favorites ‚Äî good candidates for promotions.
  • Membership affects buying patterns: customers tend to continue ordering their favorites post-membership, but there's some minor variation.
  • Tracking pre- and post-membership purchases could be useful to create loyalty programs.
